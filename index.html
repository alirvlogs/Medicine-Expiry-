Official Statement on Expired Medicines

Published: January 2025

Our Commitment to Patient Safety

At our company, patient safety is our highest priority. We believe that every person deserves access to medicines that are safe, effective, and of the highest quality. This statement explains our position on expired medicines and why they pose serious risks to public health.

Why Medicines Should Never Be Used After Expiry

Every medicine we produce has an expiry date printed clearly on its packaging. This date is determined through careful scientific testing and represents the last day we can guarantee the medicine will work as intended.

Using expired medicines is dangerous for several reasons:

  • The active ingredients may break down and lose their healing power
  • Chemical changes can create harmful substances inside the medicine
  • The medicine may not treat the illness, allowing the condition to worsen
  • Bacteria or mold may grow in liquid medicines past their expiry

Important: Selling expired medicines is illegal in most countries and puts patients at serious risk. We strongly oppose this practice and urge all medical stores to follow proper guidelines.

Health Risks and Loss of Effectiveness

When medicines expire, they undergo changes that can affect both their safety and their ability to heal. Understanding these risks is essential for everyone—patients, caregivers, and healthcare providers.

Reduced Effectiveness

Expired antibiotics may not fully kill bacteria, leading to infections that do not heal. Expired heart or blood pressure medicines may fail to control dangerous conditions. Pain relievers may not provide proper relief.

Potential Health Risks

Some expired medicines can cause allergic reactions, stomach problems, or other side effects. In rare cases, certain medicines can become toxic after expiry, causing serious harm to vital organs.

A Note on "Safety Margins": While some people believe medicines remain safe shortly after expiry, we cannot recommend this practice. The expiry date exists to protect you, and no amount of cost savings is worth risking your health.

Legal and Safety Responsibilities of Medical Stores

Medical stores and pharmacies play a critical role in protecting public health. They have both legal obligations and moral responsibilities to ensure that only safe, unexpired medicines reach patients.

1

Regular Stock Checks

All medicines must be checked regularly for expiry dates. Products nearing expiry should be moved forward or removed from shelves.

2

Proper Storage Conditions

Medicines must be stored at correct temperatures and humidity levels. Poor storage can cause medicines to expire faster than the printed date.

3

Complete Record-Keeping

Detailed records of all medicines—including batch numbers and expiry dates—must be maintained for accountability and regulatory compliance.

4

Staff Training

All pharmacy staff should be trained to understand the importance of expiry dates and the proper handling of medicines.

Proper Expiry Tracking and Safe Disposal

Preventing the use of expired medicines requires careful tracking systems and responsible disposal methods. Both healthcare facilities and individuals at home should follow these guidelines.

✓ Expiry Tracking Best Practices

  • • Organize medicines with oldest items at the front (First-In, First-Out method)
  • • Mark calendars or set reminders for monthly expiry checks
  • • Create a simple list or spreadsheet of all medicines and their expiry dates
  • • Return medicines to suppliers before they expire when possible

✓ Safe Disposal Guidelines

  • • Never throw medicines in regular household trash or flush them down toilets
  • • Use authorized medicine take-back programs in your community
  • • If no program exists, mix medicines with coffee grounds or kitty litter before disposal
  • • Remove personal information from medicine containers before discarding

The Role of Digital Systems in Prevention

Modern technology offers powerful tools to prevent expired medicines from reaching patients. We encourage all pharmacies and healthcare facilities to adopt digital inventory management systems.

Benefits of Digital Inventory Systems

Automatic Alerts: Systems can send warnings before medicines expire
Real-Time Tracking: Know exactly what stock you have at any moment
Barcode Scanning: Prevents human errors in data entry
Detailed Reports: Generate compliance reports for inspections
Sale Prevention: Block expired items from being sold at checkout
Better Planning: Order the right quantities to reduce waste

We recommend that government health authorities consider requiring digital inventory systems in all licensed pharmacies. This simple step could prevent countless cases of expired medicine usage and save lives.
